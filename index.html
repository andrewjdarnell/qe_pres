Quality Engineering

Evolving from finding bugs to building quality in.

Speaker: Andrew Darnell

Principal Test Engineer

The Traditional Approach: Quality Assurance (QA)

Concept: Reactive testing at the end of the development cycle.

  • Focuses on detecting defects after they are introduced.
  • Often a separate phase performed by a distinct, siloed team.
  • Heavy reliance on manual execution and repetitive tasks.
  • The unfortunate perception: "Quality is the QA team's responsibility."

"We can't release yet, QA is still testing."

The Evolution: Quality Engineering (QE)

Concept: Proactive integration of quality practices throughout the entire software development lifecycle.

  • The same QA skills are reused, but instead of reviewing quality just once at the end, they are turned into continuous assurance.
  • Focuses on preventing defects and improving engineering processes.
  • Integrated directly into CI/CD pipelines for continuous feedback.
  • Repetitive testing is done by machine, preserving human attention for creative testing approaches.
  • The reality: "Quality is everyone's responsibility."

"Quality is built into the pipeline, not audited at the end."

Key Differences (The Shift in Mindset)

From Traditional QA... To Quality Engineering...
Reactive finding of bugs Proactive mitigation of risk
Manual Test Execution Test Automation & Infrastructure creation
Siloed gatekeepers Embedded Quality Experts and enablers
A bottleneck to release An accelerator for speed and scale

Accelerating through Feedback Loops

  • Continuous Feedback: Building unit tests and automated checks into the CI/CD pipeline gives instant feedback to developers, helping them build better quality code from day one.
  • Shifting Left: It isn't about pushing testing onto developers. It is about Quality Engineering working collaboratively with Development to enable quality checks early.
  • Addressing Complexity: Component and integration tests address combinatorial system complexity on solid ground, reducing the need for massive end-to-end regression runs.
  • Strategic E2E: End-to-end tests remain vital for validating concert operations and critical business journeys, but shouldn't be the primary safety net.

Why Make the Shift?

For the Business (Managers)

  • More Predictable Projects: Consistent delivery velocity.
  • Faster Time to Market: Automatic regression runs.
  • Higher Confidence: Less risk in production.
  • Lower Long-Term Costs: Shift-left identifies bugs when they are cheapest to resolve.

For the Team (QAs)

  • No Scripted Regression: Let the machines do the repetitive tasks.
  • Upskill Into Engineering: Grow coding capability.
  • High-Value Scope: Focus human intellect on exploratory and design-level testing.
  • Empower the Organization: Create testing frameworks and tools.

Shared Skills & Shared Outcomes

QA and QE care about the same goals:

  • Analyzing specifications and requirements (explicit and implicit).
  • Analyzing potential risks and causes of failure.
  • Designing strategies to mitigate and reduce those risks.
  • Communicating issues and impact directly to stakeholders.

The Force Multiplier

Manual risk-based testing forces us to compromise on quality early in the lifecycle because of limited time.

Automation is like adding a parallel workforce executing high-value checks 24/7. It allows us to prioritize based on strategy, not because we ran out of hours in the sprint.

Debunking the "Too Expensive" Fallacy

  • Long-Term Math: A typical project takes 6 to 36 months to develop and runs for 5 to 10+ years in production. Monthly releases mean 72 to 120+ cycles. The upfront cost of automation pays back exponential dividends.
  • Zero Risk Appetite: Our platforms make life-changing decisions. The cost of bugs escaping into production is massive—both in system integrity and customer trust.
  • Protecting Customers: Our customers are our biggest asset. Preventing bugs in production is our ultimate duty of care.

First Line of Defence

Moving from manual judgment to systematic guardrails:

  • Old Way (QA): Relying entirely on manual tester judgment to decide what to check and what to skip under deadline pressure.
  • New Way (QE): The automated testing framework operates as the first line of defence, the last line of defence, and everything in between.
QE Rule of Thumb:

Identify where you currently limit your tests due to available human effort. That is your next automation target.

Action Plan: How Do We Go About This?

Technical Skillset

  • Start with Python: A powerful, accessible scripting language with a massive test automation ecosystem.
  • Master E2E & API: Focus on browser end-to-end automation (Playwright/Selenium) and API integration verification.
  • CI/CD Pipelines: Understand how code is built and deployed. Run tests automatically in the pipeline.

Mindset Shift

  • Think Big: If you had a virtual army of 1,000 parallel testers, what would you test? Automation gives you that capability.
  • Start Small: Solve your immediate regression bottleneck first. Take small steps.
  • Leverage AI: Use AI to assist with writing scripts where appropriate, but maintain critical thinking over the verification logic.
  • Evaluate constantly: "Are our feedback loops working?"
